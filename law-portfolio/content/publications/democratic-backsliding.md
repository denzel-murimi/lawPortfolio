This paper analyzes the challenges faced by Tanzania and the African Union in addressing democratic backsliding. It explores institutional blind spots, the effectiveness of existing legal frameworks, and the implications for governance and human rights. The study offers recommendations for strengthening democratic safeguards and enhancing regional cooperation.
